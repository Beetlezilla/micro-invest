How it works

No stock picking, no lump sums, no habit change. Buy things the way you already do.

01

Connect, read-only

Link your bank through regulated Open Banking. Access is strictly read-only — Micro Invest can see purchases, and can never move money. Revoke it at your bank, any time.

02

Spend as normal

When a purchase lands, the engine maps the shop to its listed parent company and applies your slider — say 10% of a £100 order — with a £1 minimum per investment.

03

Own a slice

Fractional shares are bought at live market prices and appear in your portfolio with full history: every purchase, every decision, and every skip with the reason why.

It knows who really owns the brand

Buying from a subsidiary invests you in the listed parent — mapped through a curated ownership graph, and skipped honestly when a merchant can't be matched with confidence.

Security & privacy, built in from day one

Micro Invest is developed security-first: every control below is implemented and in force today, in the pre-launch build — not promised for later.

Two-factor sign-in

Access to the application requires a password plus a rotating 6-digit authenticator code (TOTP). Sessions are signed and expire; failed logins are rate-limited.

Encrypted at rest — AES-256

All financial data lives in an AES-256 encrypted database (SQLCipher). The encryption key is never stored in plaintext — it is wrapped by the operating system's key-protection service.

Encrypted in transit — TLS 1.2+

Every connection — to your bank's data provider, to market data, and to the app itself — is encrypted with TLS 1.2 or better. No plain-HTTP access exists.

Zero-trust access

Nothing is trusted by network location. Every device authenticates with cryptographic keys, every user with MFA — and the network layer never grants application access by itself.

Read-only by design

Bank access is read-only via regulated Open Banking: Micro Invest cannot initiate payments or move money. Consent expires automatically (90 days) and can be revoked at any time.

Least privilege everywhere

Token- and certificate-based authentication throughout (JWTs, OAuth-pattern tokens); the one internet-facing credential is write-only in scope; the app runs without admin privileges.

Audited and patched

Dependencies are scanned against vulnerability databases on every start-up, with a published patch SLA: critical fixes within 72 hours. Access reviews run quarterly, with findings logged.

Governed by written policy

A documented access-control policy, and a Data Retention & Disposal Policy aligned with UK GDPR: data kept only while needed, deleted within 30 days of closure, never shared or sold.

One identity, instant revocation

All access is centralized on a single MFA-protected identity with per-device enrolment — disabling one identity or removing one device revokes every access path immediately, with no stray credentials left behind.

Encrypted backups

Backups are exported only as passphrase-encrypted archives (PBKDF2, 600k iterations) — useless without the passphrase, and the database key is re-bound to hardware on restore.

Where the project is today

  • Pre-launch development. The only user is the developer, testing on his own accounts.
  • Investing is fully simulated. No real money is moved, no payments are initiated, and no brokerage orders are placed. Portfolio values use live market prices; holdings are simulated.
  • Data is minimal. Merchant name, amount, and date of settled transactions — nothing else. No card numbers, no credentials, no statements.
  • Regulated path planned. Real investing will launch only with a regulated custodian partner and the appropriate FCA permissions.
